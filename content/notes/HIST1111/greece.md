# The Minoans
* Crete had Neolithic communities before the rest of europe
* they formed the Minoan Civilization
* discovered by Arthur Evans in the early 20th century
* the civilization seems to have been a single kingdom
    * Knossos
* developed a writing system called **Linear A**
* language and writing are unknown/undecyphered
* experienced a tsunami and invasion from the Greeks
* after that, they switched to writing in Linear B
* linear B was used by the Mycenaeans

# The Mycenaeans
* the name cones from the site known as Mycenae
* they appear to be the first Greeks to arrive
* Mycenaeans were warlike, but also merchant seafarers
* Mycenaean cities were independent of each other
* they cooperated a few times
    * the invation of Crete
    * the Trojan War

# Hellenic Civilization
## The Archaic Period
* The Polis
    * community of free people making up a city state
    * critical factor in greece was citizenship
    * greeks were citizens rather than subjects
    * The Agora 
        * an open area for markets, public structures, and public gatherings
    * The acropolis
        * a fortified hill
* Athens was the largest
    * around 300,000 people
* each polis was a distinct political and cultural unit
* not all residents were citizens
    * citizens were native-born, free males over the age of 20

* four types of governments in greek poleis
    * monarchy
    * oligarchy
        * aristocracy
    * tyranny
        * autocracy
    * democracy

* Athens and Sparta were the most important poleis
* Corinth and Thebes were also important
* there were many others

# Athens
* originally a monarchy
* became an aristocracy
* the oligarchy gave Solon supreme power to impose reforms
* outlawed debt slavery
* Pisistratus became a tyrant
    * numerous reforms
* Cleisthenes became a tyrant
    * founded Athenian democracy
    * Ekklesia - assembly of all citizens
        * absolute pure democracy
    * Boule
        * council of 500 citizens
        * 1 year terms
        * representative democracy
        * ran less important affairs
    * Deme
        * territorial unit
        * similar to a precinct
* Ostracism
    * exiling
    * it was a common punishment
    * lost all rights of citizenship
# Sparta
* conquered its nearest neighbors
* a new way of life was established - absolute service to the state
* Lycurgus, a legendary law-giver
* the defeated people became near-slaves - helots
* Spartan males devoted their lives to being soldiers
* Sparta's economy was met by the helots
* Spartan women oversaw farms and raised their children
* Spartan government was made up of the ephors (five officials elected annually)
    * two kings
    * an assembly of citizens 30 yo and up
    * a council of elders (included the kings and elected members of age 60 and up)
* Most greeks admired the Spartan way of life, and Spartan virtues

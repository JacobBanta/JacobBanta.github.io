neolithic and bronze age

  • warfare and invasion by nomads, Turco-Mongolian

  • millet in the north, rice in south
  • numerous neolithic cultures
  • agriculture and metalworking discovered independently

Philosophy, idea, religion

  • harmony brings social stability

  • reverence for ancestors

  • ancestors can bless their descendants

  • fortune-telling and oracles

    • oracle bones
    • Feng-shui
    • I Ching
    • etc.

Civilzation

Xia Dynasty

  • the first royal dynasty identified
  • not well known

Shang Dynasty

  • second royal dynasty identified
  • more is known about this period
  • strictly hierarchical society
    • powerful king with warrior court
    • skilled artisans small traders in towns
    • peasants
  • when kings dies, a young woman was killed to accompany the king to the spirit world
  • most were from noble families
  • important innovations:
    • bronze casting
      • very fine bronze casters
      • world renowned
    • writing
      • origionally pictographic, then developed huge vocabulary of signs called logographs
      • logographs represent ideas
      • there were around 5000 logographs needed to be literate
      • immenslet important in unifying groups which came to call themselves "Chinese"
      • earliest writing found on oracle bones

Zhou Dynasty

  • nomadic invaders from the west
  • overthrew Shang Dynasty
  • extended borders
  • extensive literature
  • Mandate of Heaven
  • powerful military men were the first rulers
  • feudal society developed
  • noble's power grew, and royal's power fell
  • central power broke down completely

Warring States Period

  • numerous kingdoms formed in the downfall of the Zhou Dynasty
  • wars and chaos broke out
  • philosophers sought a way to end the violence and promote harmony

Confucious

  • Analects attributed to him
    • education
    • behavior, manner, traditions
  • practical interests contered on ethical, correct relations
  • chinese family is the model
    • gov't's role is to create a society that functions as a harmonious family
    • each person has rights and obligations
  • gentility was the chief virtue
    • courtesy
    • justice
    • moderation
    • rich and strong had obligation to poor and weak
  • the proper role for gentlemen was in gov't
  • education would stress confucian teachings
  • civil service examination system based on confucian education
  • low opinion of merchants
  • harmony over change
  • contempt of change
  • distrust of foreigners

Daoism(Taoism)

  • concentrated on nature, following the Way
  • Lao Zi possibly wrote the Dao De Ching
  • the way of nature is percieved through meditation and observation
  • man must seek harmony and avoid all extremes
  • best gov't is least gov't
  • eventually degenerated into peasant superstition

Legalism

  • philosophy of gov't rather than private life
  • justification of applying force when persuasion fails
  • people seen as evil and selfish
  • gov't exists to make them do good
  • strict censorship
  • crush independent thought

Imperial China

Qin Dynasty

  • king Qin Shi Huang-di defeated all rivals to become China's first emperor
  • First emperor had great influence
  • centralization along legalist lines
  • weights and measures standardized
  • first standard units of money
  • writing system standardized
  • great wall and other public works projects started
  • expanded territory
  • some negative aspects
    • torture, harch treatment, consctipted labor
    • burning of the books to combat Confucianism
  • overthrow of the first emperor led to the Han dynasty

Han Dynasty

  • simultaneous with Rome, some things in common
  • non-hereditary
  • peasants were heavily taxed
  • borders expanded

Confucianism

  • new imperial confucian ideology of the state
  • blended confucianism and legalism
  • established a governance philosophy practiced by subsequent dynasties

Art and Sciences

  • history has always been important to the Chinese - careful records were kept
    • most prominent: Sima Qian
  • mathematics, geography, astronomy
  • invention of paper
  • medicine
    • acupuncture
  • fine arts
    • silk
    • bronzes
    • jade
    • ceramics
    • poetry
    • landscape painting
    • instrumental music
  • canals
  • road improved communication and commerce
  • large cities and markets
  • iron was common: new and cheaper weapons made ever-larger armies possible, expanded agricultural productivity
  • trade expanded - establishment of The Silk Road
  • government functioned through elaborate bureaucracy, members chosen by examination
  • meritocracy brought out best talent regardless of birth
  • heavy taxation led to rebellion
  • Buddhist monks and merchants from India arrived in China
  • the Han Dynasty collapsed due to invasion and regional revolts
  • regional strongmen (warlords) established themselves in small kingdoms
  • much of north china was united for most of this period

Sui Dynasty

  • reunited China
  • revived the Civil Service Exam system
  • initiated major canal building projects
  • most important was the Grand Canal which linked the Yellow river and the Yangzi River

Tang Dynasty

  • administratice reform - the empire divided into departments
  • improvements in land use; improved lives of peasants
  • scholarship, the arts, and literature flourished, especially poetry
  • whole page block printing developed
    • as opposed to the gutenburg press where the characters are placed on a plate
  • Tang government closely regulated economic activity
  • Buddhism spread rapidly throughout China
    • many monastaries founded
    • new Buddhist sects developed
  • Tang Dynasty overthrown as a result of internal uprisings and barbarian pressure to the north
  • rival genreals fought for power and formed the ten kingdoms in south china

Song Dynasty

  • defeated rivals and reunited China
  • development of moveable type printing
  • expanded tea production
  • Chinese merchant communities took shape on the South China Sea
  • Maritime trade networks were established
  • The Mongols conquered China in 1279
